# Public Domain Research — Robert E. Howard's Hyborian Age & Thurian Age

> **This file is the canonical source of truth for what we can legally use,
> verbatim or as direct adaptation, from Howard's catalogue.** All other
> lore documents (GAME_DESIGN.md §10, MODULE_PLAN.md, individual adventure
> module files) must respect what is and isn't on this list.

> **Last verified:** April 2026. Re-verify each work's status before each
> module ships to production. New works enter PD every January 1 — see the
> "Future PD Calendar" section at the bottom.

---

## Currently Public Domain (United States, as of April 2026)

The following Howard works entered public domain through **non-renewal of
their original Weird Tales magazine copyrights** and are freely usable in
Living Eamon — including verbatim quotation, character names, location
names, plot beats, and direct prose adaptation:

### The Hyborian Age (essay, 1936 — published in *The Phantagraph*, with earlier fragments)

The full world-building document detailing the prehistory, races, and
kingdoms of the setting. **This is the only Hyborian-Age-specific work
currently in PD.** It contains no characters by name, no plots, no
specific named heroes — only the broad historical, geographic, and
cultural framework.

### The Thurian Age stories (Kull cycle, 1929–1930)

Three short stories from Howard's earlier Thurian Age (the
pre-Cataclysm era that directly precedes the Hyborian Age in his
internal chronology). All three are fully PD because their original
magazine appearances had no copyright renewal:

- **"The Shadow Kingdom"** — *Weird Tales*, August 1929. Kull of
  Atlantis seizes the throne of Valusia and discovers serpent-men
  shapeshifters infiltrating the court.
- **"The Mirrors of Tuzun Thune"** — *Weird Tales*, September 1929.
  Kull visits the wizard Tuzun Thune and confronts visions of
  alternate realities and the nature of kingship.
- **"Kings of the Night"** — *Weird Tales*, November 1930. Kull is
  summoned through time to aid Bran Mak Morn, king of the Picts,
  against Roman legions; explicitly bridges the Thurian and
  Hyborian/Pictish lines.

**Why the Thurian Age matters for Living Eamon:** the Thurian Age is
where much of the "Occult" magic, items, and lore in the Hyborian Age
originally come from — the lost civilizations, the serpent-men, the
ancient sorcerers. Their **ruins** are the natural dungeon-locations
for our Hyborian-Age player to explore. This makes the Thurian PD
stories exceptionally valuable: their ruins, races, and concepts can
be the basis for adventure modules set in the player's Hyborian present.

### From "The Mirrors of Tuzun Thune"

- **Tuzun Thune** — the ancient wizard who keeps a chamber of mirrors
- **Concept:** mirrors as portals to alternate realities / visions
- **Concept:** confronting visions of alternate selves; the question

- **Bran Mak Morn** — king of the Picts
- **The Pictish lands / Pictish Wilderness** (as ancient precursor to

  ritual summons Kull through time)
- **Concept:** ancient pre-human races and forbidden knowledge

  can use the time-summoning trope without using Romans (we can swap
  in any Hyborian-era enemy — Stygian raiders, Hyrkanian horsemen,
  serpent-men, etc.).

## What Is NOT Public Domain (Yet)

**No complete Conan short stories or the novel *The Hour of the Dragon*
(1935–1936) have entered public domain in the US.** They will begin
entering starting **January 2028** onward, story by story, based on
original magazine publication dates.

This means the following remain under copyright until their individual
entry dates:

- **All Conan stories** by Howard (e.g., "The Tower of the Elephant,"
  "Queen of the Black Coast," "Red Nails," "Beyond the Black River")
- **The Hour of the Dragon** novel (1935–1936) — including Xaltotun,
  the Heart of Ahriman, and the city of Python
- **Acheron** as a named empire — ⚠️ **VERIFIED NOT PD (April 13,
  2026):** The word "Acheron" does NOT appear in the public-domain
  *Hyborian Age* essay. Howard introduced Acheron later, in the
  copyrighted novel *The Hour of the Dragon* (1935–1936), where it
  functions as the ancient sorcerous empire that once dominated the
  region later occupied by Aquilonia, Nemedia, and parts of Koth.
  **The name "Acheron" cannot be used in Living Eamon prose, item
  descriptions, scene prompts, or module copy until 2031.** Use
  **"Thurian"** (the pre-Cataclysmic age, PD-safe via the essay +
  the three Thurian-Age short stories) as the canonical equivalent.
- **Xaltotun** — same status. Introduced in *The Hour of the Dragon*.
  NOT PD until 2031. Use **Tuzun Thune** (PD via *The Mirrors of
  Tuzun Thune*) as the template ancient-wizard figure.
- **Heart of Ahriman** — same status. NOT PD until 2031. Use the
  **mirror chamber** from *The Mirrors of Tuzun Thune* as the
  template legacy artifact instead.
- **Python** as the capital city — same status, NOT PD until 2031.
- **Specific named characters** beyond Kull, Bran Mak Morn, and Tuzun
  Thune: Conan, Bêlit, Valeria, Thoth-Amon, Yag-Kosha, etc.
- **Specific magic items** invented in the protected stories
- **Detailed city descriptions** drawn from the fiction (versus the
  essay's broad geographic strokes)

The essay gives the **skeleton** of the world; the protected stories
flesh it out with protected expression.

## Future PD Calendar (US copyright timeline)

US copyright law: works published 1929 entered PD January 1, 2025;
works published 1930 entered January 1, 2026; etc. (95 years from
publication for pre-1978 works.)

**Upcoming Howard PD entries — first publication year basis:**

| Year of PD entry | Eligible Howard works (first publication year) |
|---|---|
| 2025 (already PD) | "The Shadow Kingdom" (1929), "The Mirrors of Tuzun Thune" (1929) |
| 2026 (already PD) | "Kings of the Night" (1930) |
| 2028 | First Conan stories: "The Phoenix on the Sword" (1932), "The Scarlet Citadel" (1933) — anything from 1932 |
| 2029 | More 1933 Conan stories: "Tower of the Elephant," "Black Colossus," "The Slithering Shadow," "The Pool of the Black One," "Rogues in the House," etc. |
| 2030 | 1934 Conan stories: "Queen of the Black Coast," "The Devil in Iron," "The People of the Black Circle," "A Witch Shall Be Born," "Jewels of Gwahlur," "Beyond the Black River" |
| 2031 | 1935 Conan stories: "Shadows in Zamboula," "The Hour of the Dragon" (serialized December 1935 – April 1936) — finally **Acheron, Xaltotun, Heart of Ahriman** become explicitly PD |
| 2032+ | "Red Nails" (1936) and any remaining late stories |

**Re-check renewal status carefully each January.** Some works'
copyright was renewed and stays protected through life+95; others
lapsed via non-renewal and entered earlier. Treat the table above
as a default schedule — verify each work the year before we use it.

## Adventure Module Implications

Because we have only the essay + 3 Thurian-Age stories currently PD,
**all near-term adventure modules must be built around:**

1. **The Hyborian-Age world skeleton** (kingdoms, peoples, geography
   from the essay) — used for SETTING, not for plot
2. **The Thurian-Age PD stories** — used for CHARACTERS (Kull, Bran
   Mak Morn, Tuzun Thune, Serpent Men), CONCEPTS (mirror-portals,
   shapeshifter infiltration, time-bridges), and RUINS (Valusia,
   Atlantis, the Pictish Wilderness)

The Thurian Age is a perfect fit because **its ruins are the natural
dungeon-locations for our Hyborian-Age player.** A player exploring
"the ruined towers of Valusia" is doing exactly what Howard's Conan
would have done — visiting the half-buried remnants of an older,
darker civilization.
