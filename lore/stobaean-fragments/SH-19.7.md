# SH 19.7 — On Forgetfulness

*A verse from a drinking-song the old men sing in the back of the hall — the kind of song that grows quieter the more times you've heard it.*

> Coming down, the soul drinks deep
> of the river called Forget.
> Coming down, it sleeps the sleep
> that wakes us into debt.

Hokas sets the cup down. Some men, he says, are brave enough to remember.
