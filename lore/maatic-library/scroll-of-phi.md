# The Scroll of Phi

*An ancient parchment, kept in the deepest archives of the Ma'atic Library. The hand is unknown — older than the Order itself.*

---

```
1                       Let φ be the ratio
─── = φ − 1             of the whole to the part,
 φ                      where the part stands to the whole
                        as the lesser to the greater.

                        Then the whole, divided into itself,
                        is the part, less the unity.

                        And the unity, taken from the whole,
                        is the part again.

                        This is the only number
                        that is its own reflection,
                        and its own reflection's mother.

                        Where you find it, look closely:
                        the nautilus, the sunflower, the spiral
                        of the falling star.

                        The world remembers its measure.
```

---

## On the truth of this scroll

Every word is mathematically true. **Phi (φ ≈ 1.618033988…)** is the unique positive number for which `1/φ = φ − 1`. It is the only number whose reciprocal equals itself minus one. The Greeks called it the *golden section*; the Renaissance called it the *divine proportion*; the Order calls it **the world's measure of itself.**

Phi appears, unbidden, in the spiral of the chambered nautilus, the head of a sunflower, the branching of a fern, the proportions of the human body, the arms of distant galaxies. The ratio of consecutive Fibonacci numbers approaches it asymptotically. It is the most irrational of all numbers — meaning it is the *least* expressible as any simple ratio, and thus the most *naturally* expressed by living things, which must distribute themselves across space without falling into resonance.

A hero who comprehends this scroll gains Illumination — small but real — for understanding that the world has been measuring itself since before there were eyes to see.
