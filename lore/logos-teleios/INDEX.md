# Logos Teleios — The Perfect Discourse

A codex of unknown age, found in a Pre-Thurian vault below the Old Necropolis of Ostavar. The leather is older than the binding; the binding is older than the inks. Brother Inan, who carries it now, was Order before he read it.

In the Way-of-Thoth quest line, the codex is recovered at **Step 14** during the Pre-Thurian vault expedition. Reading it triggers Scroll XIV's Illumination award and unlocks the path to Mother Khe-Anun at the Lighthouse of the Last Word.

## Provenance

The Greek title *Logos Teleios* ("Perfect Discourse") names the Hermetic dialogue more commonly known by its surviving Latin name *Asclepius*. The complete work was lost in late antiquity; what survives is the Latin *Asclepius* and a small set of Greek excerpts preserved in Stobaeus and the Coptic Nag Hammadi Codex VI (1945 discovery).

**Modern scholarly readings:** Walter Scott *Hermetica* Vol. III (1924, US-PD); G.R.S. Mead *Thrice-Greatest Hermes* Vol. II (1906, US-PD); Brian Copenhaver *Hermetica* (1992, in copyright — not used here).

**This file's prose is original Living Eamon writing.** The thematic content of the Hermetic Lament — the gods withdrawing from the land, the strange-season, the calling-home, the remaking — is genuinely ancient public domain. No modern translator's text is reproduced.
