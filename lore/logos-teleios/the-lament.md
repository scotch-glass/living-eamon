# Logos Teleios — The Lament

*A codex of unknown age, recovered from a Pre-Thurian vault below the Old Necropolis. Brother Inan opens it carefully, as one opens a door whose hinges have not turned in a thousand years. He reads aloud in the cold lamp-light.*

---

A time will come — and is already coming — when the gods withdraw their hands from the land. The temples will stand, but they will be empty rooms. Men will forget how to look up. They will call the old reverences foolish, and the old foolishnesses wise. The earth will sicken in answer. Stones will refuse their places. Beasts will speak in dreams the words men have stopped speaking awake.

The wonders that remain will be called madness; the madness that comes will be called sense. The dead will be more honored than the living, for the dead at least remembered.

This is not the end. This is the strange-season.

After the strange-season comes the calling-home — and after the calling-home, the remaking. The Word that built the world has not unmade it. It has only grown quiet. He who keeps the watch in the quiet is named in the remaking.

---

*The codex breaks off here. The remaining leaves are blank — or were left blank, deliberately, by a hand that knew when to stop writing.*

*Brother Inan closes the book. He does not speak for a long time.*
